Jairus’s Daughter and the Woman with Chronic Bleeding
(5:21–43)
We have here another ne example of intercalation in Mark’s Gospel (the
others are found in 3:20–35; 6:7–32; 11:12–26; 14:1–11; 14:54–72). e
story of the healing of the woman with a menstrual disorder is sandwiched
by the story of the raising of Jairus’s daughter.
Menstrual blood and death are regarded as rendering people ritually
impure, but Jesus overcomes this without being made impure, even though
he comes into contact with them. Hence, there is a thematic connection
with the healing of the Gerasene demoniac where there was extreme ritual
impurity. It must also be stressed that the theme of faith remains domi-
nant.

In this regard, we may claim there is also a connection with the
miracle story in 4:35–41, where the theme of faith is prominent. We shall
explain how this theme is further developed in this passage.
e Humility of Faith: the Request of Jairus (5:21–24)
e story takes place on the Jewish side of the Sea of Galilee (i.e., aer Jesus

the synagogue, but a member of the body of elders that elects the presider.
