Paul’s list includes vices the Corinthians commit. ese include the

But it is not necessary for us to connect every vice on

this list with a current problem in their church; these vice-doers represent

what the congregants used to be prior to conversion (6:11). Some once were

idolaters, adulterers, swindlers, revilers, thieves (1 Pet 4:15; John 10:10),

and passive and dominant same-sex erotic partners.

But such vices were washed away when the Corinthians accepted the

gospel, confessed Christ at baptism (Acts 22:16; Rom 10:9–10), and were

consecrated as saints (cf. 1:2) and justied by faith. ey were set in right

standing with God by the authority of the Lord Jesus Christ and by the

Spirit of our God who lled them at their conversion-initiation and con-

tinues to transform them (Gal 2:16; Rom 3:24, 26; cf. 1 Cor 12:13). e

Holy Spirit dwells in and among them, sanctifying and empowering them

to resist these vices and produce virtuous characteristics instead (cf. Gal

5:22 –23; Rom 8:1–16). is review of their conversion reinforces the Cor-

inthians to identify themselves not with the way they formerly lived when

practicing unrighteousness, but as they are supposed to be currently liv-

ing—as a righteous and holy community in Christ.
