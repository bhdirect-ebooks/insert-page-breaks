Paul the Administrator and Judgment Day (4:1 –5)

Paul continues his discourse on wisdom and apocalyptic images related to

judgment day. Some add that this section is an apology, a self-defense of

Paul’s character and ministry.

150

His explanation in 4:6, however, along with

further instructions, exhortations, and a call to imitate his behavior (4:1, 5,

14, 16–17), suggest that his primary goal remains a deliberative attempt to

change Corinthian discordant behavior.

His opening exhortation, thus let a person consider, begins a string of

words related to judgment in 4:1–5. In correction of the way he and Apollos

have been placed on pedestals to compete with one another, Paul challenges

the congregation to regard them as mere assistants and administrative

stewards of Christ.
