Of the names of seventeen members mentioned by Paul or Acts (1

Cor 1:1, 11, 14; 16:17, 19; Rom 16:1, 21–23; Acts 18:2, 7–8), nine are Greek

(Achaicus, Chloe, Crispus, Sosthenes, Stephanas, Erastus, Jason, Phoebe,

and Sosipater) and eight are Latin (Aquila, Prisca, Fortunatus, Gaius, Lu-

cius, Quartus, Tertius, and Justus).

and possibly Sosthenes, are Jews, but the church mostly consists of Gen-

names suggest that a number of congregants may be inuenced by Roman
