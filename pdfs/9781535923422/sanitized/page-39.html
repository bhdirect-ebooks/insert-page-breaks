Final salvation will be the inevitable outcome for those who are “in Christ”

(1:2, 4; cf. v. 9), that is, those who belong to and remain in the corporate

body of Christ, maintaining spiritual fellowship with him. As we will notice

in this letter, among the destructive vices threatening the Corinthians’ new

identity are divisions, idolatry, and fornication. ey are also inuenced by

the ideology of Roman elitism and civic identity, which are set in opposi-
