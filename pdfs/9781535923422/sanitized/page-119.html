In what sense is an unbelieving spouse made holy by the believing spouse?

The typical unbeliever in Corinth is not a secularist in the modern sense of

the word. He or she believes in other deities rather than the one God of Jews

and Christians. This person likely found it annoying or even sacrilegious that

the Christian spouse no longer venerated other gods and household spirits.
