God’s Solution for All (3:21–31)

Humanity has sinned and merited judgment (3:9–20), but God remains

faithful and righteous (3:21–31; cf. 3:1–8), so that people can also be

set right with him through faith. e law revealed sin but did not make

people righteous (3:20). We should not take this limitation to mean that

the law itself is bad (7:7, 14). e problem is not the law, but using it in

a way that it was never intended to be used. e law teaches right from

wrong but does not provide, nor does it claim to provide, self-justication

by one’s own religious achievement. e law and the prophets do in fact

teach the right way to be put in right relationship with God and made

righteous (3:21): not by boasting in one’s achievement, but by faith, i.e.,

depending on God (3:27, 31). Paul will go on to specify that at this stage

in salvation history the faith must be in God’s ultimate work, Jesus the

Messiah (3:26).
