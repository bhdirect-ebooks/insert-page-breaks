Sexual Sin (1:24–27)

Paul has narrated that humanity exchanged the truth about God for

idolatry (1:19–23), which he here calls a “lie,” the opposite of truth (1:25).

A direct consequence of this behavior was that God handed them over to

dele their own bodies sexually (1:24), including in same-sex intercourse,

which was “against nature” (1:26–27), i.e., (for a Jew) against the way God

created things to be. In the primeval era of the “creation” (Rom 1:20), God

revealed his character and made humanity in his image (Gen 1:26–27);

yet they distorted God’s image by worshiping other images (Rom 1:23).

83

Exchanging God’s truth for lie involved idolatry (1:23, 25), but also a per-

version of right sexuality in God’s image to distorted sexuality (1:24, 26).
