Moreover, the possibility of chaos is not far from any society. For example,

an infrastructure collapse would threaten massive death in heavily urbanized,

economically interdependent societies. Yet even without such large-scale

catastrophes, all believers face suffering—the death of a family member,

struggles with a severely autistic child, miscarriages, and so on.

Counselors warn against giving a glib assertion that “all things work for

good” to a person who is suffering. Instead, we should begin to learn to trust

Paul’s message of God’s sovereign care and destiny for us before we suffer.

At times we may be content learning such ideas without incorporating them

in our lives; when we face suffering, however, with only God to cling to, the

genuineness of our faith is tested. Then, with God’s help, we have opportunity

to show our faith, to further develop an intellectual afﬁrmation into a life of

deeper trust.
