While the preceding four seals have both similar structure and similar

content, this seal and the next have only a roughly similar structure.

Together they depict the two key groups of those who live on earth at

the time of John’s writing: the followers of the Lamb, who have been

slain by “those who dwell on the earth” (vv. 9–11); and those who dwell

on the earth themselves (vv. 12–14). e absolutely crucial matter for

the rest of the present narrative, not to mention for the book as a whole,

are the questions raised by the two groups. e martyrs ask, “How long,

Sovereign Lord, . . . until you avenge our blood?” (v. 10). e rest, on

their day of judgment, cry out, “e day of their [God and the Lamb’s]

wrath has come, and who can withstand it?” (v. 12). ese two seals,

therefore, complement each other while at the same time they prepare
