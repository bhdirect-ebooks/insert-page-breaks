who talked with John, who thus had a measuring rod of gold (what

else?) with which to measure the city, including also in this case both

its gates and its walls. And for the present reader who might easily get

lost in the measurements that follow, it needs to be pointed out at the

beginning that the “new Jerusalem” itself is a perfect cube, as high as it

is wide and long, nearly 1400 miles
