Fusing the Horizons: Progress in the Christian Life

In George MacDonald’s novel, The Princess and Curdie, the Grandmother

explains to Curdie:

When you met your father on the hill to-night, you stood and spoke

together on the same spot; and although one of you was going up

and the other coming down, at a little distance no one could have

told which was bound in one direction and which in the other. Just so

two people may be at the same spot in manners and behaviour, and

yet one may be getting better and the other worse, which is just the

greatest of all differences that could possibly exist between them.

120

Paul has urged Timothy to progress on becoming a positive model in right

doctrine, conduct, love, faith, purity, reading, encouragement, teaching,

and in use of his spiritual gift. How do contemporary pastors “progress” in

their lives as Christians?
