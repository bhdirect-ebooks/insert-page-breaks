Slaves (6:1–2)

Paul now moves from widows and elders to slaves: As many are slaves

under the yoke regard their own masters worthy of all honor, lest

God’s name and the teaching may be slandered. But the ones having

faithful masters do not despise, since they are brothers and sisters,

but more serve, since they are faithful and beloved, the ones devot-

ing themselves to good deeds (6:1–2a). Unlike Aristotle, who addresses

only masters, Paul in 6:1–2 and in Titus 2:9–10 addresses only slaves,

probably household slaves.
