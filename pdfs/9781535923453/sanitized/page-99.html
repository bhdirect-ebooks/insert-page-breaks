Augustine makes the point that, for the new covenant people, their “way”

must be unique at its core. These people do not simply receive written or

verbal teaching for intellectual consumption, but, rather, the instruction they

receive (even when through humans) is imbued with the presence and power

of the Holy Spirit itself. And the people are not simply informed by God, but

they are moved. This is why Paul can talk about the God who gives his Spirit

to the people (4:8). “Thus you will know them by their fruits” (Matt 7:20). As

we recognize the powerful work of God in us, his transformed people, may we

sing together a song of exhortation to love like this one.

Gracious Spirit, Holy Ghost

taught by you, we covet most,

of our gifts at Pentecost,

holy, heavenly love.
