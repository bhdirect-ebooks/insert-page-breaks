If we regard the impartation of the

word of Messiah as the goal of teaching, admonishing, and singing, then

we are led to the conclusion that teaching is meant to take on a worship-

ful character while musical praise is to take on a didactic role in order

to comprehensively impart the word. Christian teaching is not meant to

be dry, but soaked in thankful praise. Similarly, singing is not purposed

to be doctrinally benign but should comprise a pointer to the truth of

Jesus Christ. In the background to all of this is the notion that whatever

Christians do in worship, teaching, work, leisure, or life, they do in the

name of the Lord Jesus, giving thanks to God the Father through him.
